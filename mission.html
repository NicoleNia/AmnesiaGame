01

THE GAME

Why are we making this game?
How various types of amnesia can affect anyone.

Our main reason to create this fine tuned game started as a high-school tech project. Originally, we chose to create a Simon Says game with a twist—additionally, one of our creators suffers from dissociative amnesia (learn more about that at https://dissociation-station.carrd.co/).
With all of this in mind, it was difficult to focus for that creator, let alone do work. Our goal with this game was to raise awareness to the struggle that many amnesiacs or memory-issue individuals suffer with on an everyday basis.
Following Babina, our main character and point-of-view, they have retrograde amnesia. This means that she loses existing memories—like how to decode her nightly documents. There are many types of amnesia, some of which will be listed below.












02

THE FACTS

What issue does this address?


Babina's amnesia is not one-size-fits-all.

As mentioned before, Babina has retrograde amnesia, meaning that they are losing existing, past memories. Below is a list of the types of amnesia.

  • Retrograde amnesia: The loss of existing, previously made memories. Retrograde amnesia generally affects recently formed memories, compared to childhood memories.
  • Anterograde amnesia: The inability to form new memories. Anterograde Amnesia is generally temporary, caused by blackouts from head injuries or alcohol consumption.
  • Transient global amnesia (TGA): A developmental condition that you likely will not have memory of. TGA causes agitation/confusion for hours.
  • Infantile amnesia: The inability to recall the early years of your life. Most people do not remember the first 3-5 years of their life.
  • Post-traumatic amnesia (PTA): Amnesia induced by trauma. This usually occurs after unconsciousness—you often see this in TV shows.
  • Drug-induced amnesia: Amnesia induced by certain drugs.
  • Dissociative amnesia: Amnesia induced by a dissociative disorder, generally experiencing a disconnection between reality, identity, thoughts, memories, etc., as a reaction to trauma.
