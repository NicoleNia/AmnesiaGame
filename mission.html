THE GAME

Amnesia can affect anyone.

The creation of this game stammed from a high-school technology project. We had originally chosen to create a Simon Says game with a twist, but it evolved due to the inspiration of one of our creators.
The creator, who suffers from dissociative amnesia, frequently found it difficult to focus, an acute limitation in a work-heavy school environment. Our goal with this game was to raise awareness to the struggle that many amnesiacs or memory-issue individuals suffer with on an everyday basis.
Following Babina, our main character and point-of-view, players experince the reality of retrograde amnesia; however, there are several types of amnesia, all different and unique. So, how do we tell each apart?

THE FACTS

[Babina's] amnesia is not one-size-fits-all.

As previosly menioned, players play through [Babina], a security guard with retrograde amnesia, a neurological condition that causes patients to lose existing, past memories; however, it is important to be able to discren the different types of amnesia as each is uniquely different and disparate. Below is a list of the several types of amnesia.

  • Retrograde amnesia: The loss of existing, previously made memories. Retrograde amnesia generally affects recently formed memories, compared to childhood memories.
  • Anterograde amnesia: The inability to form new memories. Anterograde Amnesia is generally temporary, caused by blackouts from head injuries or alcohol consumption.
  • Transient global amnesia (TGA): A developmental condition that you likely will not have memory of. TGA causes agitation/confusion for hours.
  • Infantile amnesia: The inability to recall the early years of your life. Most people do not remember the first 3-5 years of their life.
  • Post-traumatic amnesia (PTA): Amnesia induced by trauma. This usually occurs after unconsciousness—you often see this in TV shows.
  • Drug-induced amnesia: Amnesia induced by certain drugs.
  • Dissociative amnesia: Amnesia induced by a dissociative disorder, generally experiencing a disconnection between reality, identity, thoughts, memories, etc., as a reaction to trauma.

THE MISSION


*Our Purpose*

Is to spread awareness about amnesia to display the frustrations and struggles of living with the disease; Amnesia is a general term for memory loss. This is not just a struggle for the afflicted individual in question, but the friends, family, occupation, and even safety of others in some instances. Our team is devoted to providing a simulation of what dealing with amnesia is like in a work environment.
