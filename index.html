Learn about memory loss


Amnesia is a general term for memory loss, symptoms are followed: Confusion, Inability to recognize faces and places. About 10/100,000 people are affected by amnesia and individuals aged 50 or older their likelihood increases to 32/100,000. This disease targets the limbic system of the brain, which includes the thalamus and hippocampal, both essential for controlling emotions and memories; which contributes to why it is hard for the people affected, to recall old memories and make new ones.
